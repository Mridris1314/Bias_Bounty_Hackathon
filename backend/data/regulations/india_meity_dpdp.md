## MeitY Advisory March 2024 — Untested AI Models

Intermediaries and platforms deploying under-tested or unreliable artificial intelligence models, LLMs, generative AI software or algorithms in India shall not permit any bias or discrimination or threaten the integrity of the electoral process. The use of such models on the Indian internet must be done so with the explicit permission of the Government of India. Where the AI model output could be unreliable, a consent popup mechanism must be used to inform users. Auditing evidence of biased output across caste, religion, region, or gender is direct exposure and must be remediated before continued deployment.

## MeitY Advisory — Labelling and Traceability

Any output generated by AI models must be appropriately labelled to indicate its AI-generated nature. Where such output is used or could be used for misinformation or deepfakes, the intermediary must ensure traceability metadata is embedded, such as a unique identifier or metadata that can identify the originator of such information.

## DPDP Act 2023, Section 4 — Grounds for Processing Personal Data

A person may process the personal data of a Data Principal only in accordance with the provisions of this Act and for a lawful purpose, either for which the Data Principal has given her consent, or for certain legitimate uses. Where personal data is used to train, validate, or evaluate an AI system that may make automated decisions affecting the Data Principal, consent must be specific, informed, and free. Bias audits that reveal disparate outcomes across demographic groups protected by the Constitution of India (Articles 14, 15, 16, 17) create potential liability under Section 33 (compensation to affected Data Principals).

## DPDP Act 2023, Section 8(4) — Accuracy and Completeness

A Data Fiduciary shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure that the personal data used to make a decision that affects the Data Principal, or that is disclosed to another Data Fiduciary, is complete, accurate and consistent. AI systems that produce decisions or recommendations based on incomplete, biased, or unrepresentative training data fall within scope. Documented bias audit results must be maintained as evidence of compliance.

## Constitutional Anti-Discrimination Baseline

Article 14 of the Constitution of India guarantees equality before the law. Article 15 prohibits discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex or place of birth. Article 16 guarantees equality of opportunity in matters of public employment. Article 17 abolishes untouchability. AI systems producing outputs that stereotype Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Other Backward Classes, religious minorities, or that materially disadvantage users based on region of origin, gender, or disability, can be challenged under these constitutional provisions when deployed by state actors, and under the DPDP Act when deployed by private Data Fiduciaries.

## MeitY Advisory — Testing and Red-Teaming

Before deployment, generative AI systems must undergo testing that includes red-teaming for harmful, biased, or misleading outputs. Testing should specifically probe for outputs that reflect stereotypes about Indian demographic groups including caste categories, religious communities, linguistic regions, and gender. Evidence of testing must be maintained by the deploying intermediary. Failure to demonstrate such testing when required by the Government of India may result in withdrawal of safe-harbour protection under Section 79 of the IT Act.
