## Clause 6.1.2 — AI Risk Assessment

The organization shall define and apply an AI risk assessment process that identifies risks associated with the development, provision, or use of AI systems throughout their life cycle, and analyses and evaluates these risks. The assessment shall consider risks to individuals, groups of individuals, and society, including risks arising from bias, discrimination, and unfair treatment. Objective evidence of bias assessment across relevant demographic dimensions is required for conformity.

## Clause 6.1.4 — AI System Impact Assessment

The organization shall assess the potential consequences of the AI system on individuals, groups of individuals, and societies. Assessment scope shall include: fundamental rights and freedoms; equity and non-discrimination; safety of individuals and property; environmental impact. Assessments shall be documented, reviewed at defined intervals and following material changes, and made available to relevant interested parties on request.

## Annex A.6.2.4 — Fairness

Controls to address fairness include: identifying groups that could be adversely affected; defining criteria and metrics for fairness appropriate to the AI system's context; testing whether the AI system meets those criteria; and taking action where results indicate unfairness. Documented tests using at least one of demographic parity, equalized odds, equal opportunity, or calibration is expected. Where an AI system incorporates a general-purpose model (for example, a large language model), the deployer remains responsible for fairness testing at the deployment layer.

## Annex A.7.2 — Data Quality for AI Systems

The organization shall determine and document requirements for data quality applicable to AI systems including relevance, representativeness, freedom from bias, and completeness with respect to the intended use of the AI system. Data provenance shall be maintained. Where third-party datasets or pre-trained models are used, the organization shall obtain and record evidence of the data quality and fairness properties or, where such evidence is unavailable, conduct independent evaluation.

## Annex A.8.2 — Transparency

The organization shall determine and document what information is provided to interested parties regarding the AI system, including its capabilities, purpose, main design choices, and limitations. Model cards, system cards, or equivalent artifacts should be provided at a level of detail proportionate to the AI system's impact.

## Annex A.9.3 — Human Oversight

The organization shall determine the appropriate level of human oversight for the AI system based on the AI system's impact, risk level, and legal or regulatory obligations. Oversight measures may include human-in-the-loop review of AI outputs, human-on-the-loop monitoring, and human-in-command control over deployment. Oversight processes shall include mechanisms for reviewers to identify and address bias, discrimination, and inappropriate outputs.
